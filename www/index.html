Observability for AI Workloads on HPC

Beyond GPU Utilization Metrics

Samuel Desseaux - Erythix / Aureonis

"Your GPU is at 95% utilization. Great. But is your model actually learning?"

The Problem

Most HPC and AI teams monitor infrastructure metrics: GPU utilization, memory usage, power draw. These metrics can look perfectly healthy while your training is completely broken.

Why Traditional Monitoring Fails for AI

What You See

  • GPU Utilization: 92%
  • VRAM Usage: 85%
  • Temperature: 72C
  • Power: 350W

Everything looks healthy!

What's Actually Happening

  • Throughput dropped 40%
  • Data loading at 45% wait time
  • Loss stopped decreasing
  • Wasting $$$$ on GPU hours

Training is broken!

The Three Layers of AI Observability

Effective AI observability requires monitoring at three distinct layers, with correlation between them.

Layer 1: Infrastructure

What we already do

  • dcgm_fi_dev_gpu_util GPU utilization
  • dcgm_fi_dev_fb_used VRAM usage
  • dcgm_fi_dev_power_usage Power draw
  • dcgm_fi_dev_tensor_active Tensor cores

Layer 2: Workload Efficiency

What we miss

  • training_samples_per_second Real throughput
  • training_data_loading_ratio I/O bottleneck
  • training_model_flops_utilization MFU
  • training_all_reduce_time Sync overhead

Layer 3: Model Health

What we ignore

  • training_loss Loss curves
  • training_gradient_norm Gradient magnitude
  • training_convergence_velocity Learning rate
  • training_perplexity Model quality

Cross-layer correlation is the game changer.
When GPU util is high but throughput drops, you have a hidden bottleneck.

Key Metrics Explained

Metric Why It Matters Alert Threshold
training_model_flops_utilization MFU is the gold standard - shows how much of theoretical GPU performance you're using < 30% is concerning
training_data_loading_ratio Time spent waiting for data vs training. Hidden I/O bottlenecks appear here > 15% indicates bottleneck
training_gradient_norm Detects exploding/vanishing gradients before they crash training > 100 = explosion, < 0.01 = vanishing
training_convergence_velocity Rate of loss decrease. Negative means model is getting worse < 0 for sustained period
training_samples_per_second Real training velocity. Compare against your baseline 40% drop from baseline
